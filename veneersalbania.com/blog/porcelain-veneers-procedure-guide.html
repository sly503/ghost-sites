Porcelain Veneers: Step-by-Step Procedure Guide

Published 14 March 2026 • 6 min read

If you're considering porcelain veneers and want to know exactly what the process involves — from your first message to walking out with your new smile — this guide covers every stage. We've written it specifically for patients travelling to Tirana, Albania, so the timeline and logistics reflect what our partner clinics actually deliver.

Quick summary: Most patients complete their full veneer treatment in Albania within 3–4 days. This includes consultation, digital smile design, tooth preparation, temporary veneers, and final fitting of your permanent porcelain veneers.

Step-by-Step: The Veneer Process

1

Online Pre-Consultation (Before You Travel)

Share photos of your smile via WhatsApp. The treatment coordinator and dentist review your case and confirm whether veneers are suitable. You'll receive a personalised quote and a clear treatment plan before booking flights. No surprises on arrival.

2

Day 1 — Full Clinical Consultation & Digital Smile Design

On arrival in Tirana, your first appointment covers X-rays, an oral health check, and a digital smile design session. Using smile-simulation software, you'll preview exactly how your veneers will look before any tooth is touched. Most patients find this appointment takes 60–90 minutes.

3

Day 1 (afternoon) — Tooth Preparation

A thin layer of enamel (typically 0.3–0.7mm) is removed from the front surface of each tooth. Local anaesthetic is used — you won't feel pain. Impressions (or a digital scan) are then taken of your prepared teeth. This is sent to the in-house or partner ceramic laboratory overnight.

4

Day 1 (end) — Temporary Veneers Fitted

Before you leave the clinic, temporary veneers are bonded to your prepared teeth. These protect your teeth and give you a preview of your new smile shape. You'll wear these for 1–2 days while your permanent veneers are made.

5

Day 2–3 — Lab Fabrication

Your permanent veneers are hand-crafted in the laboratory. Our partner clinics use E.max lithium disilicate ceramic — the same material used in leading UK and European practices. The lab works from precise digital impressions to match your approved smile design.

6

Day 3–4 — Final Fitting & Bonding

The dentist removes the temporaries, cleans the prepared teeth, and tries in your permanent veneers for fit and colour. You'll be asked to check the look and feel before anything is permanently bonded. Once you're happy, a special dental adhesive bonds each veneer securely. Final polish and bite check complete the session.

Does It Hurt?

The preparation stage involves local anaesthetic, so you should feel pressure but no pain during the procedure. Some patients experience mild sensitivity for a few days after preparation, particularly to cold. This typically resolves once permanent veneers are placed.

Temporary veneers may feel slightly different to your natural teeth — this is normal. Avoid hard or sticky foods while wearing them.

What Type of Veneer Is Right for You?

  • E.max porcelain: The gold standard for aesthetics. Thin, strong, and highly lifelike. Best for patients wanting the most natural result.
  • Zirconia: Extremely durable. Slightly less translucent than E.max but excellent for patients who grind their teeth.
  • Composite (direct): Applied in a single visit, no lab required. Good for minor corrections. Less durable than porcelain — typically lasts 5–7 years vs 15+ for porcelain.

Who Is a Good Candidate for Veneers?

Veneers are suitable for patients who want to address:

  • Discolouration that doesn't respond to whitening
  • Chipped, cracked, or worn teeth
  • Mild spacing or gaps between front teeth
  • Uneven or irregular tooth shape
  • Teeth that appear too small or too large

Veneers are not suitable for patients with active gum disease, significant tooth decay, or severely misaligned teeth requiring orthodontic treatment first. The pre-consultation process identifies any of these issues before you travel.

Aftercare: How to Make Your Veneers Last

With proper care, porcelain veneers last 15–20 years. Key aftercare rules:

  • Brush twice daily with a non-abrasive toothpaste
  • Floss daily — veneers don't protect gum health
  • Avoid biting hard objects (ice, pens, nails)
  • Use a night guard if you grind your teeth
  • Attend routine check-ups (these can be with your UK dentist)
  • Limit coffee, tea, and red wine where possible — porcelain is stain-resistant but not stain-proof

Frequently Asked Questions

Do veneers damage your teeth?

The preparation process removes a small amount of enamel, which is irreversible. This is why veneers are considered a permanent commitment. However, the preparation is minimal (typically 0.3–0.7mm) and does not compromise tooth strength when done correctly.

Can I eat normally after veneers?

Yes — once permanent veneers are bonded, you can eat normally. Avoid very hard foods (ice, hard sweets) and anything that could chip the porcelain.

What if something goes wrong after I return home?

Our partner clinics provide written guarantees and have English-speaking aftercare coordinators reachable via WhatsApp. For minor issues (loose veneer, sensitivity), your UK dentist can assist with emergency care. We coordinate with you directly if anything needs attention.

How soon after getting veneers can I fly home?

Most patients fly home the same day as their final fitting appointment or the morning after. There is no clinical reason to wait — you leave Tirana with your permanent veneers already bonded.
